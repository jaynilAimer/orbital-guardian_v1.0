THE SITUATION — WHY THIS TERMINAL EXISTS

    
On 10 February 2009, the operational communications satellite Iridium-33 collided with the derelict Russian military satellite Cosmos-2251 at a relative velocity of 11.7 km/s, producing over 2,300 tracked fragments. On 11 January 2007, China's anti-satellite test against its own Fengyun-1C weather satellite created more than 3,500 pieces of trackable debris — the single worst contamination event in orbit history. Those fragments are still up there, crossing the paths of India's NavIC constellation and Japan's QZSS fleet every 90 minutes. This terminal exists because the sky is a shared resource under active threat, and sovereign operators need the fastest possible intelligence on what is approaching their spacecraft.

PHYSICS & MODELS

        
    
          
  • SGP4/SDP4 — Vallado (2013), Fundamentals of Astrodynamics and Applications. Simplified General Perturbations propagator matching NORAD elements.
    • 
          
  • Foster-1992 — Foster & Estes, NASA JSC-25898. 2-D encounter-plane collision probability.
    • 
          
  • ML Risk Scoring — Feature-weighted heuristic (Pc, proximity, velocity × geopolitical weight). Not a trained neural network — documented openly.
    • 
          
  • Kessler Cascade — Kessler & Cour-Palais (1978) kinetic-gas model with per-shell population dynamics.
    • 
          
  • Clohessy–Wiltshire — Along-track ΔV maneuver planning with Tsiolkovsky fuel estimation.
    • 
          
  • CCSDS 508.0-B-1 — Conjunction Data Message standard for inter-agency exchange.
    • 
        

      

      

        
DATA INTEGRITY

        
    
          
  • Every TLE line is checksum-verified on load — any tampered or corrupted line is rejected and reported.
    • 
          
  • Orbital figures in the UI are never taken from the satellite dossier database — always computed live from TLEs via SGP4.
    • 
          
  • Facts not officially published by the operating agency are shown as "—" — never guessed or fabricated.
    • 
          
  • The satellite dossier matches by official name (regex), not hand-typed catalog numbers.
    • 
          
  • CelesTrak live refresh is attempted at boot; failure falls back to the embedded snapshot honestly labelled as such.
    • 
        

      

      

        
HONEST GAPS

        
    
          
  • TLE + SGP4 accuracy is ~1–3 km — not sufficient for operational maneuver decisions.
    • 
          
  • Collision probabilities use assumed covariances, not radar-fitted per-object uncertainty.
    • 
          
  • Real operations require owner/operator ephemerides, legal CDM exchange (Space-Track, EU SST), and radar data — this is a screening and training layer.
    • 
          
  • The ML risk model is a weighted heuristic, not a trained neural network with validation metrics.
    • 
          
  • ISRO's IS4OM system uses classified data and dedicated radar — this terminal uses public TLEs only.
